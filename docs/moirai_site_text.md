## Curriculum
**Heading:** Two levels. One finished film.

Both levels cover Directing, Editing, and Scriptwriting — but Intermediate isn't a continuation, it's a different depth. Where Beginner builds your foundation, Intermediate breaks down the craft: advanced actor direction, complex editing principles, world-building in the script, and a full Producing track covering budgets, locations, and production logistics.

### Beginner

**Track:** Directing
*See the world through a director's eye.*
From the earliest images in cinema history to the principles that still govern every shot made today. You'll learn to read and construct images — composition, framing, the power of observation — and understand how a director builds meaning before the camera even rolls.
Topics: Visual language · Composition & framing · Shot sizes · Voiceover & dialogue

**Track:** Editing
*Where films are made twice.*
Editing is not just assembly — it's storytelling. You'll discover how the sequence of images creates emotion, tension, and meaning, then get hands-on with the tools used on real productions.
Topics: How cuts create meaning · Rhythm & pacing · Editing software

**Track:** Scriptwriting
*Every great film starts on the page.*
Learn the fundamental architecture of a story — how acts, characters, and conflict work together to drive a screenplay forward. You'll write scenes from scratch, including monologue and dialogue.
Topics: Three-act structure · Character & conflict · Writing for the screen

**Final Project — Beginner**
### Your First Short Film
Shot, edited, and submitted by you. Reviewed personally by both instructors.

### Intermediate

**Track:** Directing
*Lead the room. Lead the set.*
The full scope of what a director actually does on a professional production — including the parts no one talks about.
Topics: Storyboarding · Action & pacing · Working with actors · The director's script

**Track:** Editing
*Ten principles that change how you cut.*
A deep dive into the rules — and how to break them. You'll work through the core principles that define professional post-production, then apply them in a final hands-on exercise.
Topics: 10 principles of editing · Rhythm & structure · Final edit project

**Track:** Scriptwriting
*Build a world worth filming.*
Move beyond structure into atmosphere. You'll develop the world of your story — its tone, logic, and texture — and finish the track with a polished, production-ready script.
Topics: World-building & lore · Final script draft

**Track:** Producing
*Film is a business. Know the numbers.*
The practical side of making films — how much things actually cost, how to find and lock locations, and how to manage a budget without losing creative control.
Topics: Budget breakdowns · Locations & logistics · Crew payments

**Final Project — Intermediate**
### Festival-Ready Short Film
A complete short film built to submit to festivals. Reviewed in a dedicated session with both instructors.

## FAQ

**Q: Is this fully online? What time are sessions held?**
A: Yes, fully online via Zoom. All sessions are scheduled in Eastern Time (ET). If you're in a different timezone, all sessions are recorded so you can watch on your own schedule and submit assignments at your own pace.

**Q: Do I need any prior film experience?**
A: No — Beginner is designed for complete newcomers. A smartphone is enough. Intermediate is for students who have completed Beginner or have equivalent hands-on experience.

**Q: Do I need to take Beginner before Intermediate?**
A: Yes, Intermediate builds directly on Beginner. If you've already made films and want to skip ahead, reach out — we'll assess your level and let you know if you're ready.

**Q: How long are the live sessions?**
A: Each session is approximately 90 minutes: lecture, examples, and open Q&A. All sessions are recorded for students who can't attend live.

**Q: What camera do I need?**
A: A smartphone is completely fine. The focus is on thinking and storytelling, not expensive equipment.

**Q: What is the final project?**
A: In Beginner: a short film shot, edited, and submitted by you — reviewed personally by both instructors. In Intermediate: a festival-ready short film with a dedicated review session and guidance on where to submit it.

**Q: How much time per week?**
A: Around 3–5 hours: sessions across three parallel tracks plus weekly assignments. Designed to be manageable alongside school or a full-time job.

**Q: When does the next cohort start?**
A: Beginner and Intermediate cohorts run on separate schedules. Apply and we'll confirm the next available date for your level. Spots are limited to 15 students per cohort.
