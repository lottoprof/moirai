## The Director Is a Generalist

> And that's not a weakness — it's the job

- The creative part is the smallest part.
- Most of your time is logistics, communication, and decisions.
- You don't need to be an expert in every department.
- You need to **speak their language**.

---

## Pre-Production

> Where the film is actually made

### 6-Week Timeline for a Short Film

| Weeks | Focus |
|-------|-------|
| **1–2** | Lock the concept. Write the script. Give it to someone else to read — you cannot see your own blind spots. |
| **3–4** | Casting + locations simultaneously. Storyboards and director's script. Don't skip the storyboards. |
| **5–6** | Final confirmations: locations, cast, crew, budget. First read-through. Then shoot. |

> Pre-production is where decisions live.

---

## Production & Post

### On Set
> Your job is oversight — not execution

- The DP manages camera. The gaffer manages light. **You manage outcomes.**
- Run through blocking with actors in the actual space — every time.
- Call sheet goes out the evening before every shoot day.

### Post-Production

**Sound Design**
Shapes the emotional experience as much as the image. Budget for it. Be in every session. Do not leave it to the last week.

**Color Grading**
Not "fixing" the footage — a creative decision. Give your colorist a visual brief. It can save a difficult shoot or destroy a beautiful one.

## From Practice

A friend finished shooting and handed everything to a skilled editor.  
Two weeks later: he hated the cut. The editor made all the right technical decisions and none of the right creative ones — because no one explained what the film was supposed to feel like.  
Four more weeks in the editing room, starting over. Budget gone.

> The director who doesn't understand post loses the film in the edit — when it's too late to go back.
