## What Is a Director's Script?

> Not a screenplay. An operations manual.

- A **screenplay** tells the story.
- A **director's script** films it.

Everything that needs to be coordinated lives here: actors, crew, props, camera, light, sound.  
When everyone reads the same document — they work from the same vision.

---

## What Goes Inside

> Six elements — every shot, every scene

| Elements | Content |
|----------|---------|
| **1–2** General information | Title, format, runtime, mood, goal of each scene |
| **3–4** Action & character | What physically happens, emotional state, micro-actions |
| **5–6** Sound & edit | Dialogue, ambient sound, music cues, cut type, rhythm between shots |
| Location & light | Interior/exterior, time of day, lighting direction, color temperature |
| Technical notes | VFX, department-specific notes, constraints |
| Shot structure | Number, type (wide/medium/close/POV), movement, composition, duration |

## Real Example

**Scene:** She tells him she's leaving.

| Shot | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Shot 1** | Wide, static. Both in frame. Ordinary world about to change. |
| **Shot 2** | Medium on her, slight low angle. She speaks. ~30 sec. |
| **Shot 3** | Detail: his hands on the table. Stillness. ~8 sec. |
| **Shot 4** | Close on his face. No response yet. ~15 sec. |

> **Note for cinematographer:** natural light only, cool, from window stage left.

## Why It Protects You

> Continuity. Budget. Your memory.

### Continuity
- Scenes shoot over multiple days.
- Costume, prop, actor must match exactly.
- Without notes: errors, reshoots, lost days.

### Budget Tool
- Shot-by-shot planning shows what gear you actually need.
- Most beginners over-rent from anxiety.
- A director's script lets you rent precisely.

---

## From Practice

**What Happens Without a Director's Script**

A student production had no script and no prop tracking.  
On day two, the main character's jacket — worn in scenes from day one — had been left elsewhere. No one had photographed it.  
Half the day lost. One scene had to be rewritten.

> A director's script is continuity insurance. Cheap to build, expensive to skip.

---

> Your director's script won't survive reality perfectly.  
> But the director who walks in with one always makes a better film than the director who doesn't.

## Practice (10 min)

**Build a Director's Script Together**

**Scenario:** a person arrives home after a long day and finds a note on the table.

1. Decide together what the note says — this determines the emotional logic of every shot.
2. Plan Shot 1: how do we see them come in? Shot type? Camera position?
3. Build at least 3 shots. Add department notes as you go.
