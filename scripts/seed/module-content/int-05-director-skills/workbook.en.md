## LECTURE SUMMARY

The director is a manager. Their job: make decisions, hold the vision, and create the conditions in which every other person can do their work.

Authority is built before the first shooting day — through three habits:

- **Be Prepared** — always have an answer, or a time when you will have one
- **Be On Time** — this is a form of respect for other people's time
- **Follow Through** — if you said Thursday, do it Wednesday

Two languages on set:

- Technical crew: precision, specifics, deadlines
- Actors: the internal world of the character, feeling — not instruction

---

## KEY CONCEPTS

**Authority on set** — not a title, but trust. Built through consistent behavior before the shoot begins.

**Two languages** — you tell the DP "camera left, backlight," you tell the actor "you love this person and you're about to lie to them."

**Conflict management** — always in private, never in public. Goal: finish the film, not win the argument.

## REAL EXAMPLE — STRUCTURE OF A FIRST EMAIL TO YOUR CREW

The director's first email to the team is the first act of establishing authority. Below is a real structure used in professional productions:

```
Subject: [Project Title] — Welcome to the Team

Hi [Name],

Glad you're on board. Here's what you need to know right now:

THE PROJECT:
[One sentence about what the film is]
Format: short film / [runtime]
Shoot dates: [dates]
Location: [city/neighborhood]

WHAT I NEED FROM YOU BEFORE THE SHOOT:
— Confirm your participation by [date]
— Send your emergency contact
— Read the attached script

HOW I WORK:
— Call sheet goes out the night before, no later than 9pm
— I reply to messages within 24 hours
— For urgent questions — call, don't text

Questions — write me. Happy to have you.

[Director's name]
[Phone number]
```

**Why this works:** concrete information, clear working method, respect for other people's time.

---

## GLOSSARY

**Call Sheet** — document sent to the entire team the night before a shoot day. Contains: address, schedule, cast and crew, technical notes.

**First AD (First Assistant Director)** — manages the schedule and daily logistics of the set.

**Read-Through** — reading the script together with actors before shooting begins. Used to establish the world of the story.

**Blocking** — planning the movement of actors within a scene. Set at the actual location before filming.
