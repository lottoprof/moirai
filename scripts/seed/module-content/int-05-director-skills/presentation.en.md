## Who Is a Director?

> **Reality check: the fantasy vs the actual job**

- **The fantasy:** a chair and a megaphone.
- **The reality:** you are the manager. Everyone else is the specialist.
- **Your job:** make decisions, hold the vision, and make every other person's work possible.

> **90%** of directing is logistics, communication, and decisions.  
> The creative vision is real — but it's the smallest part of your working hours.

---

## Building Authority

> It starts before you ever say 'action'

### Three Habits That Build Trust

**1. Be Prepared**
Always have an answer. If you don't — commit to a time when you will. Then actually follow through.

**2. Be On Time**
If the read-through is at 2pm, be there at 1:45. Every time you're late, you chip away at trust.

**3. Follow Up**
If you said you would send the call sheet on Thursday, send it on Thursday. Always.

> Your authority begins in your first email.

## From Practice

**The Real Cost of Showing Up Unprepared**

The director forgot to order the necessary equipment for the shoot day. The team had to make do with whatever was available.  
Instead of three hours, they ended up having to shoot for five. Consequently, the director lost the team's trust — a loss that carried over to every subsequent shoot.

> Authority is not something you seize. It is granted — and just as quickly taken back.  
> A single ill-prepared day can undo weeks of good work.

## Two Languages

> Crew and actors don't speak the same one

### Technical Crew
- Communicates in precision: what, where, when
- Give clear information. Be brief.
- Never explain feelings — explain outcomes.

### Actors
- Communicate in emotion and imagination
- Give them the internal world — not the external action
- Never say "stand here, look sad." Say **why**.

> Switch between them within the same minute.

---

## Conflict & Self-Care

> The set runs on your energy

### Managing Conflict
- Take it aside. Never in front of others.
- Find out what the real issue is.
- Your only goal is to finish the film — everything else is secondary.

## Practice (10 min)

**Three Real Situations:**

1. An actor has been late 3 times — each time an hour. What do you do?
2. A location was paid. Morning of shoot — it's partly off-limits. What now?
3. You have a fever. Shoot tomorrow, 8 people, rented space. What do you do?

> What does it mean to be 'the director' among people you already know?
