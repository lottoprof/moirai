# Locations, Props & Payments
**Moirai — Keep this document**

---

## LECTURE SUMMARY

The explication is the first production document — written before anything else begins. It describes the visual and emotional world of the film: atmosphere, color, light, sound, costume direction, reference films. It synchronizes all departments before production starts.

Location scouting: always bring your DP. Check light, noise, space for equipment. Photos lie — go in person. Always have a backup location.

Props: minimum 3 copies of anything used, consumed, or damaged in a scene. Photograph every costume at the start of every shooting day.

Payments: schedule before production begins, announced publicly, followed through without exceptions.

---

## KEY CONCEPTS

**Explication** — document describing the world of the film for all departments.

**Continuity** — consistency of costumes and props across shooting days.

**Payment Sheet** — list of every person, amount, and payment date.

## GLOSSARY

**Explication** — pre-production document describing the visual and emotional world of the film. Written before production begins.

**Continuity** — consistency of all visual elements across shooting days.

**W-9 Form** — US tax form for freelancers. Required for payments over $600.

**Shoot Permit** — permission to film in a public space. Required in most US cities.

**Prop Master** — crew member responsible for all props throughout production.
