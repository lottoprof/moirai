## Key Techniques

### Split Screen
A technique in which the frame is divided into two or more sections, each displaying a separate image or scene simultaneously. Used to show two or more events happening at the same time in different locations.

**Examples:**
- [Split Screen — example 1](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AcfuCp2I3PM)
- [24 — Multi Screen Sample](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y7HwSZi4AL8)

---

### Multiple Exposure
A technique in which several images are layered onto a single frame. Used to show two or more simultaneous events, convey a character's inner state, or create a smooth transition between scenes.

**Examples:**
- [True Detective — Season 1 Opening Credits (HBO)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Xyu_MdKBXic)
- [Vertigo — Dream Sequence](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7OUoOufFqaA)

## Assignment: The Méliès Frame

### Rules

1. **Static camera only**, preferably on a tripod. Panning, tilting, and changes in angle or frame size are not allowed.
2. **At least one primitive special effect is required** — such as: freeze frame, multiple exposure, split screen, fast/slow motion, etc. The number of effects used is up to the director.
3. **Every special effect must serve the story.** Its use should be motivated and purposeful.
4. **No dialogue as the central focus.** Actors and extras may speak on camera, but their speech should not be the main subject of the exercise — emphasis is on visual storytelling.
5. **Sound design and music are permitted.**
6. **Runtime: 1 to 2 minutes.**
