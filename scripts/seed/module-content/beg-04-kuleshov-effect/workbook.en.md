# Directing 4: The Kuleshov Effect

## Lev Kuleshov

Lev Kuleshov was a Soviet film director and educator whose montage experiments fundamentally shaped the way modern cinema is shot and edited.

---

## Key Concepts

### The Kuleshov Effect
A principle of film editing in which the same shot takes on a different meaning depending on the shots that surround it. First demonstrated by Lev Kuleshov in 1918.

- [Kuleshov Effect — earliest available example](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_gGl3LJ7vHc)
- [Hitchcock on Editing and the Kuleshov Effect](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DjSr7QMppi4)

---

### Creative Geography
A montage principle that allows footage shot in entirely different locations to read as a single, unified space.

- [Creative Geography — Kuleshov experiment](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KDcKr7Chqk4)

---

### Creative Anatomy
A montage principle that allows body parts filmed on different people to read as belonging to a single character.
