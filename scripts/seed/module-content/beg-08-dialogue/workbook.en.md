# Scriptwriting: Monologue & Dialogue

## Monologue

A monologue is an extended speech delivered by a single character, without an immediate response from others. Unlike dialogue, where lines alternate between characters, a monologue belongs to one speaker alone.

### Types of Monologue

1. **External monologue** — The character speaks on screen. Their words may be addressed to other characters or directly to the audience.
2. **Internal monologue** — The character does not speak on screen, but the audience hears their voice (see: voice-over).

### Examples

1. [Andor — Luthen Rael's Monologue (Disney+)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-3RCme2zZRY) — A classic example of an external monologue. One scene, one space, every word spoken aloud.
2. [American Psycho — Ending Monologue](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cuTmyIOQIhw) — An internal monologue: the character's words are heard only by the audience.
3. [Noah — Creation Sequence (2014)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FFCXHr8aKDk) — An external monologue not confined to a single space or time. Scenes shift in service of the story being told.
4. [The Big Lebowski — The Stranger's Monologue](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dZxxMg-FCt4) — A monologue that breaks the fourth wall. A character who has served as a detached observer throughout the film turns to address the audience directly, stepping into the role of narrator.

---

## Voice-Over

Voice-over is a voice heard outside the visible space of the frame. The audience hears it, but its source is not shown on screen. Unlike an internal monologue, a voice-over may belong to an outside observer with no direct involvement in the events of the film. Most commonly used in documentary, though it also appears in narrative fiction.

### Examples

1. [So Long and Thanks for All the Fish — The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N_dUmDBfp6k) — A narrator's voice-over in a fiction film: the speaker comments on events without participating in them directly.
2. [World's Grumpiest Cat — Frozen Planet II (BBC)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kgrV3_g9rYY) — Voice-over narration in documentary filmmaking.

---

## Dialogue

Dialogue is a conversation between two or more characters. Each character uses their lines to interact with the other, responding to what has been said.

### Types of Dialogue

1. **Expository** — Used to convey information the audience needs in order to understand the plot and the characters.
2. **Functional** — Used to advance the plot; shifts the direction of the story and has a direct impact on the characters.
3. **Atmospheric** — Does not advance the plot, but reveals the inner world of the characters, introducing the audience to their personalities, behavioural patterns, likes, dislikes, etc.

> Note: dialogue types are not mutually exclusive. A single scene can combine several functions — for example, an expository dialogue may also serve an atmospheric purpose.

### Examples

1. [Casablanca — Major Strasser Questions Rick](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QzQzVUCCxmA) — A classic example of expository dialogue.
2. [The Godfather — Opening Scene](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eZHsmb4ezEk) — Functional dialogue: the balance of power is established, characters are revealed, and the plot moves forward.
3. [Lost in Translation — I'm Stuck](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fDMj776smAA) — Atmospheric dialogue: the characters reflect on life, revealing their personalities without advancing the plot.
