## Types of Actors

> Know who you're working with before they walk in

### Three categories — each with their own logic

**Professionals**
- Know technique. Take direction well.
- Rarely work for free.
- Higher risk of ego — especially early career.

**Students & Amateurs**
- Trained enough to follow direction. Still learning — flexible.
- Great value for short films.

**Nonprofessionals**
- Occasionally magnetic — pure instinct. Most are unprepared.
- Plan for the rule, not the exception.

## Before the Audition

> Know exactly who you're looking for

- Review the **portfolio** first — not the headshot.
- Decide: what is the one quality you cannot compromise on? This clarity will save hours.

### The Cold Read
Give them something they haven't prepared.  
Do they panic — or do they find something real?  
That moment tells you more than any prepared piece.

---

## On Set

> What actually works when the camera's rolling

### What Doesn't Work
> "Stand here, say the line, look sad."

— This is stage management, not directing.  
— The actor performs — they don't live it.

### What Does Work
> "You haven't slept in three days. You love this person. And you're about to lie to them."

— Give them the internal world. Let them find the rest.

> **"Do not tell the actor what to do. Tell them what is true for their character in this moment."**

## Practice (10 min)

**Casting Exercise:**

1. You receive a scene and three actor profiles — who do you cast and why?
2. Compare choices with the group: where does everyone agree? Where not?
3. What do the disagreements reveal about how subjective casting actually is?
