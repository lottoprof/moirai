# Working with an Actor
**Moirai — Keep this document**

---

## LECTURE SUMMARY

Three types of actors — professionals, students/amateurs, non-professionals — require different approaches and different amounts of time.

Audition: review the portfolio, start with conversation, end with a cold read. The cold read reveals more than any rehearsed piece.

Direction: never tell an actor what to do — tell them what is true for the character in this moment.

---

## KEY CONCEPTS

**Direction through state** — instead of "stand here and look sad" → "you haven't slept in three days, you love this person, and you're about to lie to them."

**Cold Read** — the actor receives material they've never seen and performs it immediately. Reveals instinct.

**Read-through euphoria** — a great read-through creates excitement. Don't mistake that for readiness.

## REAL EXAMPLE — AUDITION PROTOCOL

This is a working document. Use it as a template:

```
AUDITION — [Project Title]
Role: [Character]
Date: [Date]
Location: [Address / Zoom]

WHAT WE'RE LOOKING FOR:
[Three qualities that are non-negotiable for this role]

SESSION STRUCTURE (30 minutes):

0:00–0:05  CONVERSATION
  — "Tell me a little about yourself"
  — "How do you understand this character?"
  — "What does your character want most in this scene?"
  Listen to how they think, not just what they say.

0:05–0:15  PREPARED PIECE
  — Give the scene 10 minutes before the session
  — After: one specific direction about the character's state
  — Watch: do they take the direction?

0:15–0:25  COLD READ
  — Different scene, seeing it for the first time
  — No preparation time
  — Watch: do they panic, or find something real?

0:25–0:30  QUESTIONS
  — "Is there anything about this role that concerns you?"
  — "Your availability for [shoot dates]?"

ACTOR CARD (fill in immediately after):

Name: _______________
Phone: _______________
Prepared piece:  1  2  3  4  5
Cold read:       1  2  3  4  5
Takes direction: yes / partially / no
First impression: _______________
Moving forward:  yes / maybe / no
```

## REAL EXAMPLE — DIRECTOR'S NOTE FORMAT

Format used by directors to record notes between takes:

```
Scene 7, Take 3:
→ Rushing the opening lines
→ Direction given: "You're afraid — but you're not showing it. Hold it."
→ Take 4 result: better, but the ending is weak
→ Take 5: try a different entrance — come in slower
```

## GLOSSARY

**Cold Read** — performing material without any preparation. Reveals instinct and adaptability.

**Self-Tape** — a video audition recorded by the actor at home and submitted digitally. Standard in modern casting.

**Backstory** — the character's history before the events of the film. A tool for working with actors.

**Method Acting (Stanislavski)** — an approach in which the actor draws on personal emotional memory to create authentic responses.

**Coverage** — filming a scene from multiple angles. Gives the editor options.
