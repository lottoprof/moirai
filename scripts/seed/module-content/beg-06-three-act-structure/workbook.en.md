# Scriptwriting 1: Drama & Three-Act Structure

## Dramaturgy

Dramaturgy is the art of constructing a story — a system of principles by which events and characters are organised. At the heart of dramaturgy is one or more conflicts that drive the story toward an inevitable conclusion.

---

## Types of Conflict

1. **External conflict** — The character struggles against outside forces: another character, a group of people, nature, or other external circumstances.
2. **Internal conflict** — The character struggles against themselves: fears, confusion, moral dilemmas, or other inner circumstances.

Both types of conflict can be expressed in different ways — spoken aloud by characters through dialogue or monologue, or shown through their actions. Conflict always has consequences: it drives characters to act, and those actions may resolve the situation or make it worse.

---

## The Three-Act Structure

A widely accepted framework that divides a story into three acts: setup, confrontation, and resolution. It provides a structure that audiences can intuitively follow.

### The Three Acts

1. **Act 1 — Setup** — The story begins. The world and its characters are introduced, and the central conflict emerges.
2. **Act 2 — Confrontation** — The conflict deepens. The action develops and the characters are tested and changed.
3. **Act 3 — Resolution** — The conflict reaches its peak and is resolved.

> Note: resolution does not always favour the protagonist. The conflict may be resolved in favour of the forces — internal or external — that created it.

### Films with a Clear Three-Act Structure

- *Star Wars* (1977)
- *Back to the Future* (1985)
- *The Matrix* (1999)
- *Pirates of the Caribbean* (2003)
- *Iron Man* (2008)

---

## Exposition

The delivery of information within a story that the audience needs in order to understand the plot. Most commonly used in the first act as a precursor to the setup. Exposition establishes the circumstances that preceded the story, the origins of the conflict, and the factors that will complicate it going forward.

---

## Character Arc

The path of a character's change over the course of the story. A character's personality, appearance, and actions may all shift as the story moves toward its conclusion. A story may have one central arc or several running in parallel.
