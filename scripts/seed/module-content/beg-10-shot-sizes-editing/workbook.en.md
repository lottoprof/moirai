# Editing: Shot Sizes

## The Six Shot Sizes (Kuleshov)

![Six Frame Sizes](six-frame-sizes.png)

1. **Extreme Long Shot** — The human figure is extremely small, occupying no more than 1/7 of the frame. Emphasis is on the location; commonly used for establishing shots.
2. **Long Shot** — The full human figure fits within the frame, with a small amount of space above the head and below the feet.
3. **Medium Shot** — The figure is framed from the knees up. Works well when characters are moving through space.
4. **Medium Close-Up Shot** — The figure is framed just above the waist. Ideal for dialogue scenes between characters.
5. **Close-Up Shot** — The head (sometimes head and shoulders) fills the frame. Most commonly used to capture important emotional reactions.
6. **Extreme Close-Up / Detail Shot** — A single eye or comparably sized detail fills the frame.

---

## The Core Principle of Editing by Shot Size

Shots of different sizes should be connected gradually, without abrupt jumps. For example, a long shot is followed by a medium shot, which is then followed by a close-up or detail shot.
