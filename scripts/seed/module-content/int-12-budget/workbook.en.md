# How to Calculate a Budget
**Moirai — Keep this document**

---

## LECTURE SUMMARY

The first budget is a maximum estimate — the highest reasonable cost for everything. It's a map of risk, not a shopping list. Then you work down from there.

Two columns always: Ideal (as envisioned) and Minimum Viable (the lowest cost at which the film still works).

15% reserve — mandatory. Do not touch unless something has actually gone wrong.

Costs people always forget: extra hours, equipment damage deposits, hard drives, crew food, transportation, permits, prop duplicates.

---

## KEY CONCEPTS

**Ideal vs Minimum Viable** — the gap between them is your negotiating room.

**15% Reserve** — for genuine emergencies. Not for things you forgot to budget.

**Above / Below the Line** — above: director, producer, actors. Below: everything else.

## GLOSSARY

**Above the Line** — director, producer, screenwriter, principal actors. Determined before production begins.

**Below the Line** — technical crew, equipment, locations, post-production.

**Contingency / Reserve** — 10–15% of the total budget set aside for unexpected costs. Mandatory.

**Day Rate** — standard daily fee for a crew member or vendor.

**W-9 Form** — US tax form for freelancers. Required for payments over $600.

**Deferred Payment** — crew agrees to be paid after the film generates revenue. Must be put in writing before production starts.
